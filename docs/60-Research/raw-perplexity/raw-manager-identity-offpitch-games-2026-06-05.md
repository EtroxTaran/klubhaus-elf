## Prompt

In football/sports management video games, how do they model a MANAGER's or coach's identity and
playstyle BEYOND on-pitch tactics — i.e. across youth development, transfer-market behaviour,
financial discipline, club-building/infrastructure investment, and crisis/resilience? Cover Football
Manager (manager attributes, 'reputation', 'preferred formation', tendencies), EA Sports FC / FIFA
manager mode, Out of the Park Baseball (OOTP), Eastside Hockey Manager, and any sim that profiles a
GM/manager's playstyle. Separately: in the REAL world of football, how are managers/sporting directors
characterised by these same dimensions (e.g. 'youth-focused', 'wheeler-dealer', 'financially prudent',
'project-builder')? I want both the game-mechanical representations and the real-world vocabulary used
by analysts/journalists. Give concrete examples and cite sources.

## Response (key points)

In most modern management sims, a manager/GM is modeled as a bundle of **hidden and visible
attributes**, **tendencies**, and **reputation** that drive AI behavior in scouting, youth
development, transfers, finances, and long-term team building, in addition to match tactics. In real
football, journalists use a parallel vocabulary—**youth-focused**, **wheeler-dealers**, **financially
prudent**, **project builders**, **short-term fire-fighters**—and those labels map cleanly onto what
the games encode. [3]

### Game-mechanical representations

- **Football Manager:** manager = data object with **attributes** (*Working With Youngsters*, *Judging
  Player Ability/Potential*, *Man Management*, *Discipline*, *Financial Control*, *Negotiating*),
  **tendencies** (Preferred Formation, Playing Style, Signing Preferences, rotation vs stable XI), and
  **Local/National/Continental/World reputation** governing hiring, board trust and player response.
  Youth = *Working With Youngsters* + club "Vision" (e.g. "Sign players under 23"). Finance = board
  budgets + philosophies + *Financial Control*. Club-building = mostly board-driven, unlocked by
  reputation/success. Crisis/resilience = no explicit stat; emergent from *Determination*, *Media
  Handling*, *Professionalism* and board confidence ("losing the dressing room"). Community language:
  a manager's **"Game Model"** as a blueprint across phases, recruitment and vision. [3][1]
- **EA Sports FC / FIFA Career:** lighter — manager is avatar + **Manager Rating / Board Confidence**
  measured against five objective categories (youth development, brand exposure, financial, domestic,
  continental). Youth/finance are **board objectives + budget constraints**, not personality; no
  persistent manager personality carries between clubs beyond rating + trophies. Crisis flattened into
  the rating bar (sacked if too low).
- **OOTP:** distinct **Managers** and **GMs** with **Personality** ratings (*Loyalty, Greed, Patience,
  Adaptability, Work Ethic*) + strategy profiles (small-ball vs power; prospects vs veterans). GMs have
  a **Trading Personality** ("win now" / "balanced" / "rebuilding"); **Owner Philosophy** (lenient vs
  tight) gates budgets. Franchise-building via scouting/development budgets + international academies.
  Job security = **Owner Patience** vs goals.
- **Eastside Hockey Manager:** managers/coaches have *Offensive/Defensive/Goalie Coaching*, *Working
  With Youngsters*, *Tactical Knowledge*, *Discipline*; GMs have *Judging Ability/Potential*,
  *Negotiating*; salary-cap discipline + *Greed/Loyalty*. Persistent successful GMs reshape org
  identity ("draft-and-develop" vs "win-via-trades").
- **Franchise Hockey Manager / basketball sims (Draft Day Sports, Basketball GM):** GM tendencies
  (pace, 3-pt focus, star-centric vs depth) + patience/risk traits shape roster behavior over years.

**Consistent pattern:** manager/GM profile = **numeric skills + temperament/personality + reputation +
contextual constraints (owner/board philosophy)** → distinct AI behaviour over seasons.

### Real-world analyst/journalist vocabulary (maps to the same dimensions)

| Real-world label | Vocabulary | Examples | Game-style parameters |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Youth-focused / developer** | "promotes youth", "develops young players", "pathway to first team" | Wenger, Pochettino; Monchi ("buy young, sell high") | high *Working With Youngsters* / *Judging Potential*; "Sign U23" vision |
| **Wheeler-dealer / market operator** | "wheeler-dealer", "transfer guru", "master trader" | Redknapp, Monchi, Marotta (free-transfer coups) | high *Negotiating*, active transfer AI, "sign to sell" |
| **Financially prudent / sustainable** | "Moneyball", "sustainable model", "within tight budgets" | Brentford (Benham), Brighton (Bloom/Ashworth), Wenger post-Emirates | high *Financial Control*; strict board wage/fee limits |
| **Project-builder / architect** | "the project", "long-term identity", "culture builder" | Klopp (Dortmund/Liverpool), Guardiola (club-wide game model), Rangnick (RB Leipzig) | long tenure, aligned board vision, facility emphasis |
| **Fire-fighter / crisis manager** | "relegation specialist", "short-term fixer", "survival" | Allardyce, Pulis, Warnock | high *Motivation/Discipline*, risk-averse, low long-term trust |
| **Ideologue / system coach** | "principle-driven", "Sarriball/Bielsaball" | Bielsa, Sarri, (early) Guardiola | fixed formation, strong style tendencies, low flexibility |
| **Pragmatist / chameleon** | "results-first", "adapts to players" | Mourinho, Ancelotti | balanced attributes, high tactical adaptability |
| **Sporting-director recruitment ID** | "data-driven", "buy low/sell high", "network builder" | Luis Campos (Monaco/Lille), Edu (Arsenal) | DOF AI: defines what kind of club you are in the market |

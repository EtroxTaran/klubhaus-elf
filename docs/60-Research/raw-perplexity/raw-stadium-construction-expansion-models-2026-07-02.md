# RAW — Stadium construction & expansion models

Raw capture for the Wave-2 dual-mode/parity research packet on stadium
construction and expansion. Queries were run 2026-07-02 via Perplexity (Sonar)
and Exa web search. Perplexity answers are condensed to the substantive,
URL-backed parts; claims whose URLs could not be independently opened are
marked in the synthesis note's confidence ratings. All club/venue names below
are real-world citations used as research evidence only — in-game names stay
IP-clean per GD-0015.

## Query 1 — Anstoss 3 Stadionbau: elements, mechanics, why beloved (Perplexity)

**Query:** Anstoss 3 (2000, Ascaron) stadium builder: buildable elements list;
expansion mechanics (grid/plots, stand-by-stand, capacity steps, construction
time, roofs, seats vs standing); why retro reviews consider it beloved.

**Substantive answer (condensed):**

- Anstoss 3 uses a **modular, tile/plot-based construction screen**: pitch and
  four independently upgradable stands (Nord/Süd/Ost/West) in the centre, with
  surrounding plots for add-on buildings. A **Stadionfundament** (new
  foundation) is bought for complete rebuilds and caps future expansion
  potential.
- Per-stand parameters: size level (small/medium/large capacity steps),
  **Stehplätze vs Sitzplätze** (standing vs seats), **Überdachung** (roof),
  VIP areas. Asymmetric stadiums (one modern side, three basic terraces) are a
  documented optimisation pattern in fan forums.
- Grounds/attraction buildings named across sources: **Würstchenbude/Imbiss**
  (sausage/snack stand), **Bierstand/Bierzelt**, **Fanshop**, **Parkplätze**,
  **Disco**, **Hotel**, **Restaurant/Kneipe**, training-ground structures
  (e.g. the "Hügel"), plus commercial elements (Werbebanden, VIP-Logen).
  Catering/merch buildings raise matchday income and fan satisfaction.
- **Construction takes time**: ordered stands/buildings complete only after
  multiple matchdays/weeks; major projects (new foundation, large stand) span
  part of a season and temporarily reduce usable capacity; fan forums factor
  "time to build" into Rentabilität (payback) discussions.
- Why beloved per retrospectives/forums: (a) you *visually* watch the complex
  grow (toy-like, concrete); (b) the builder is **economically meaningful, not
  cosmetic** — stand config, seat mix and add-on buildings visibly move income
  and attendance; (c) humorous flavour buildings (disco, hotel) give identity;
  (d) long-term progression arc from modest ground to self-designed arena.
  Contemporaries used numeric stadium sliders / fixed levels instead.
- Caveat noted by the model itself: no complete authoritative element/number
  table exists publicly; the list is reconstructed from manuals, forums and
  let's plays (some elements like business seats inferred).

## Query 2 — FM board-driven model + USM98 + We Are Football (Perplexity)

**Query:** How does stadium/facility expansion work in Football Manager
(FM20–FM24); criticism as passive/board-driven; contrast with Ultimate Soccer
Manager 98 and We Are Football (2021).

**Substantive answer (condensed):**

- **FM20–FM24:** the player **cannot design or place anything**. The only
  interaction is a board request ("Expand stadium" / "Build a new stadium" /
  "Improve training|youth facilities") via Club Vision → Make Board Request.
  The board decides yes/no, sets cost, capacity and timeline; the player waits
  for a news item. Preconditions per community guides: stadium ~25 years old
  for a new build, strong finances, regular sell-outs, board confidence
  (fminside guide: "Keep trying, keep asking and eventually you will get your
  new stadium!").
- Facilities are a **single abstract quality level** ("Good" → "State of the
  Art") plus cost/time — no module choice, no financing choice, no layout.
- Dominant community criticism: **"one-button management"** — click request,
  wait for a pop-up; no design phase, no negotiation over capacity/hospitality,
  no further interaction after facilities are maxed. Long-save players call
  the club-building fantasy capped.
- **USM 98** contrast: stand-by-stand construction (capacity, seating vs
  terracing, roof per stand), player-built executive boxes / shops / bars /
  restaurants, per-project cost+time, tight coupling to budgets and loans.
- **We Are Football (2021, Gerald Köhler):** player directly plans stadium and
  club-grounds modules with costs; positioned as club-GM fantasy rather than
  head coach.

**Cited sources:**

- https://fminside.net/guides/basic-guides/33-how-to-build-a-new-stadium-in-fm
- https://www.reddit.com/r/footballmanagergames/comments/1bc8b7c/how_to_expand_the_stadium/
- https://www.fmscout.com/q-22857-Stadium-Moves--Not-by-building-a-new-stadium.html

## Query 3 — Real-world expansion economics anchors (Perplexity)

**Query:** European stadium expansion economics 5k–60k capacity: construction
duration, cost per seat, financing instruments, capacity step sizes,
hospitality share, standing/seating ratios.

**Substantive answer (condensed):**

- **Duration:** single-stand expansion ≈ 18–36 months of main works (Liverpool
  Main Stand +8,500: Jan 2015 → 2016-17 season ≈ 20–21 months; Anfield Road
  Stand +7,000–7,800: main build ≈ 24–30 months to 2023-24). New 40k–60k
  stadium ≈ 36–60 months of main construction (Tottenham Hotspur Stadium
  62,850: ≈ 3–4 years above-ground, 4–5 years total; Bernabéu renovation
  ≈ 4–5 years of major works while in use).
- **Cost per seat:** simple end-stand mostly-GA expansion ≈ €2,000–4,000/seat;
  stand with some hospitality ≈ €3,000–6,000; major "super stand" with large
  hospitality ≈ €6,000–10,000; new modern stadium ≈ €4,000–8,000/seat
  (continental 20k–40k) up to £8,000–15,000/seat (London flagship).
- **Financing instruments actually used:** syndicated bank loans / project
  finance secured on future matchday+commercial income (Emirates); stadium
  bonds incl. German retail **Fananleihe** (small-denomination fan bonds,
  ~€5–25m raises, e.g. St. Pauli, Mainz 05); naming rights covering a large
  share of capital cost over 10–20 years (Emirates, Allianz Arena, Etihad);
  owner equity / private placements (Tottenham); premium-seat licences /
  debentures (Arsenal).
- **Capacity steps:** minor +1,000–3,000 (rail-seat conversion, corner infill);
  new end stand +3,000–7,000; major side stand +5,000–10,000; new-stadium
  jumps +10,000–30,000 (Goodison ~39k → Everton Stadium ~mid-50k).
- **Hospitality:** premium/hospitality ≈ 5–15% of capacity at modern venues but
  ≈ 40–60% of matchday revenue (FT reporting on Tottenham/Arsenal/Real Madrid
  stadium economics); premium seat yields ~5–10× a standard ticket.
- **Standing/seating:** Dortmund Südtribüne = 24,454 standing domestically,
  ~15,000 seats in UEFA all-seater config → **≈1.6× density factor** for safe
  standing; standing share ≈ 20–35% of capacity at traditional Bundesliga
  grounds, higher (30–50%) at traditional second-tier grounds.

## Query 4 — Tycoon-genre depth: fun vs busywork (Exa)

**Query:** design analyses/postmortems of construction-economy management games
on which depth is fun vs tedious busywork.

**Substantive results:**

- Craig Perko, "Directed Sandbox (Planet Coaster)" —
  http://projectperko.blogspot.com/2016/11/directed-sandbox-planet-coaster.html
  — once the economic layer is solved, the park "runs itself" and building
  becomes pure expression; economic pressure is front-loaded and then vanishes.
  Lesson: builders need either continuing economic relevance or an explicit
  sandbox framing.
- Polylusion, "What makes a great tycoon game" —
  https://polylusion.com/blog/what-makes-great-tycoon-game — failure modes:
  "too thin" (no second hour, loop collapses into busywork) and "no
  progression" (tenth hour identical to first: "You build a bigger park but
  you are doing exactly the same actions... just more of them"); success
  pattern: fast loop, visible cause→effect, layered complexity, mastery
  rewarded with new toys (Two Point Hospital tier unlocks).
- Habrador, Parkitect analysis —
  https://blog.habrador.com/2019/05/parkitect-management-game.html — opaque
  cause-and-effect (empty free ride, unexplained guest complaints) is the main
  frustration; readable "why is this underperforming" feedback is what keeps
  management depth from feeling arbitrary.
- Game Developer, "What should we do with mediocre gameplay depth in
  management games?" —
  https://www.gamedeveloper.com/design/what-should-we-do-with-mediocre-gameplay-depth-in-management-games-
  — "In most tycoon games, constructions have minimal impact on each other,
  and gameplay revolves around managing a multitude of parameters" — i.e.
  parameter-count depth without interaction is the busywork trap.

## Query 5 — We Are Football / FIFA Manager stadium reception (Exa)

**Query:** WAF (2021) reviews on stadium building depth; FIFA Manager stadium
editor/infrastructure reception.

**Substantive results:**

- GameStar test (https://www.gamestar.de/artikel/we-are-football-test-steam-review,3370516.html):
  recommends WAF explicitly for players for whom "der Aufbau von Stadion und
  Vereinsgelände großen Spaß macht" — stadium/grounds build-out named as a
  headline strength; also notes Anstoss 3 still counts to many German fans as
  the best football manager ever.
- Videospielhalbwissen (https://videospielhalbwissen.de/we-are-football-anstoss-oder-abpfiff/):
  stadium/grounds build-out is "sehr gelungen und schick dazu" and is a
  feature "von alten Anstoss-Veteranen beim FM oft vermisst" (often missed by
  Anstoss veterans in FM); budget-constrained investment choices at poor clubs
  called genuinely challenging.
- PC Games test (https://www.pcgames.de/We-Are-Football-Spiel-73258/Tests/Fussball-Manager-Gerald-Koehler-Gameplay-Review-1374412/):
  criticises WAF because "Neue Gebäude verfügen über keine Bauzeit, sondern
  sind direkt voll funktionstüchtig" — instant, build-time-less construction
  reads as missing genre standard; delegation to staff is the escape hatch.
- MobyGames, FIFA Manager 10 (https://www.mobygames.com/game/46675/fifa-manager-10/):
  "the player sets the number of toilets, VIP rooms and similar things" —
  documented example of micro-granular stadium infrastructure; the same series
  offered per-aspect difficulty/responsibility toggles (delegate whole areas),
  an early per-area easy/pro split.
- WayTooManyGames WAF review (https://waytoomany.games/2021/06/17/review-we-are-football/):
  praises "realistic stadia, club grounds and training centers ... letting you
  explore them at will" as a retained FIFA-Manager strength.

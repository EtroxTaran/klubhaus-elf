# Raw capture — national-team dual-role in management sims

> **`status: raw`.** Verbatim Perplexity (Sonar) capture for FMX-84. Synthesised in
> [[../national-team-dual-role-2026-06-06]]. Not implementation authority.

## Prompt

> In football/sports management simulation video games, how do they handle the
> player-manager taking on a NATIONAL TEAM job in addition to or instead of a club job?
> Compare specifically: (1) Football Manager (Sports Interactive) — can you manage a club
> and a nation simultaneously? How do you get offered international jobs
> (reputation/nation/results)? How does it handle the international calendar and
> club-vs-country fixture conflicts? (2) FIFA Manager / EA, and older titles like
> Championship Manager. (3) Out of the Park Baseball (OOTP) and other sports sims with
> national-team or all-star duties. (4) The Anstoss series (German football manager games)
> and how they treated the 'Bundestrainer' national coach role. For each, describe how the
> dual-role is gated/unlocked, whether both jobs run at once, and the engagement/automation
> options offered to the player. Cite sources.

## Response (verbatim)

In most football and sports-management sims, you *can* take a national-team job **alongside**
a club job, but the way it is unlocked, scheduled, and automated varies a lot by series and era.

### 1. Football Manager (Sports Interactive)
- **Dual roles supported**: manage a club and a national team at the same time; coaches can also
  be employed by both concurrently.
- **Getting an international job**: start the save as a national coach at setup; apply via the
  Job Centre / Job Security screens when vacant; or receive unsolicited offers when manager
  reputation + recent results are strong. Inferred factors: manager reputation (results, level,
  longevity), nationality & language (own-nation FAs far more likely to target you), recent
  success (leagues, continental wins, over-performance).
- **Calendar & conflicts**: international fixtures pre-scheduled (qualifiers, friendlies,
  tournaments); call-up reminders → squad → matchday lineup. Players on international duty are
  unavailable for clubs; FM respects FIFA dates and pauses leagues where appropriate; lower
  divisions that play through FIFA dates lose called-up players. As a club manager you can
  request rest from *other* national managers but **cannot rest players in the national team you
  yourself manage**.
- **Engagement/automation**: delegate squad/lineup selection to staff (assistant), delegate
  briefings/opposition instructions/press. International management is widely criticised as
  structurally **shallow** vs club play — lots of dead time between breaks, fewer squad-building
  tools — so many run a nation purely for tournaments and sim/delegate the rest. You can play
  as a national-team-only manager, but FM treats it more as a "side activity".

### 2. FIFA Manager (EA) & Championship Manager
- **FIFA Manager (08–14)**: club + national simultaneously in Career mode in many versions
  (e.g. 12–14), or national-only. Start directly as a national coach (weaker nations) or be
  offered the job after club success; reputation = team success + league level + history (formula
  unpublished). International breaks/tournaments integrated into the season calendar; leagues pause
  where appropriate. **Simpler than FM**: less scouting/tactical detail; delegate match control
  (sim with/without highlights), let assistants handle friendlies, or decline national offers.
- **Championship Manager (classic SI-era CM 2 / 97-98 / 01-02)**: manage a nation and club
  simultaneously; offers from an FA on strong club performance, or start as national manager in
  some DBs. Simpler mechanics, smaller international DBs, manual call-ups/lineups, minimal
  delegation — the national job is "occasional extra matchdays".

### 3. OOTP & other sports sims
- **OOTP Baseball**: models national teams (World Baseball Classic-style) and all-star teams;
  recent versions let you manage those squads while running a club. Roles are **part-time,
  event-driven** (tournament/all-star windows). Assigned automatically by AI/league rules or via
  commissioner tools. Leagues build mid-season all-star breaks; WBC-style events have tournament
  windows. Fully manage or quick-sim.
- **NBA 2K MyGM/MyLeague**: All-Star Game control if selected — a single-weekend detour, not a
  parallel role.
- **Eastside Hockey Manager (EHM, also SI)**: dual club + national management; national jobs
  reputation-gated, scheduled in IIHF windows, heavy staff delegation available.

### 4. Anstoss series (Bundestrainer)
- **Dual role**: across the classic titles (Anstoss 2, Anstoss 3) you could manage a **club** and
  the **German national team** at the same time; the "Bundestrainer" post is a prestigious
  secondary role earned on top of the club job.
- **Getting the job**: start as a club manager; after good results (titles, promotions, high
  league finishes) receive a DFB offer to become Bundestrainer; sometimes apply when vacant. Key
  gate = success/reputation in German football (mid-table rarely leads to the national job).
- **Calendar & conflicts**: Bundesliga + cup fixtures plus international dates; as Bundestrainer
  international matches appear in your calendar (pick squads/lineups); for major windows/tournaments
  the game **pauses the domestic league** (mirroring WC/EURO breaks); club players unavailable when
  called up. Simulation less strict / more stylised than FM.
- **Engagement/automation**: choose squads/lineups/tactics, decide friendlies and tournament prep;
  limited delegation in older titles (mostly manual), but few international matches keep the
  workload manageable. Famous for humorous presentation (press/cutscenes/narrative events) that
  makes the national job feel like a **rewarded prestige role** — leading Germany at a World Cup is
  a clear **end-game fantasy**.

### Comparative summary (as returned)

| Series | Dual role? | How obtained | Calendar/conflict | Engagement/automation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Football Manager | Yes (full) | Job Centre + unsolicited offers (reputation/results/nationality) | Full real-world calendar; FIFA windows/tournaments; players unavailable on duty | Detailed but criticised as structurally shallow; delegate to staff, sim friendlies |
| FIFA Manager (EA) | Yes (club+nat or nat-only) | Start as low-tier nat coach or offered after club success; reputation-gated | Integrated calendar; breaks/tournaments pause/overlap leagues | Simpler than FM; sim matches, delegate |
| Championship Manager | Yes (classic SI-era) | Offers on strong club performance; sometimes selectable at start | Fixed calendar; players unavailable on call-up | Limited delegation; occasional extra match stream |
| OOTP Baseball | Yes (club + nat/all-star) | Auto-assigned by league/tournament rules or commissioner | Separate tournament windows + all-star breaks | Highly configurable; full-manage or quick-sim; event-driven |
| Anstoss (Bundestrainer) | Yes (club + German NT) | Earned by club success / German-football reputation | Intl fixtures slotted in; domestic play pauses for major tournaments | Manual, light workload; framed as a prestige "side job" / end-game fantasy |

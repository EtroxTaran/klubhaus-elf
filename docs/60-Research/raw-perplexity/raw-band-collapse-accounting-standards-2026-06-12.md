# Raw capture — Real-world range recognition (Perplexity, 2026-06-12)

Verbatim-faithful capture of the Perplexity strand on how real accounting standards convert range
estimates to one recognized amount, for FMX-149 (ADR-0101 D2). Synthesis in
[[../moneyband-amountminor-collapse-rule-2026-06-12]].

## Prompt (summary)

Asked for precisely sourced practice on: (1) IAS 37 best estimate for ranges (midpoint? expected
value for populations?); (2) ASC 450 — minimum of range when no better estimate?; (3) when
deliberate conservatism (low-end) is appropriate; (4) football-club treatment of contingent
transfer amounts (add-ons, sell-ons) under UEFA licensing.

## Key findings (faithful condensation)

### (1) IAS 37 — best estimate

- Provision measured at the **best estimate** of the expenditure required to settle the obligation
  (the amount the entity would rationally pay at reporting date).
- **Range with all outcomes equally likely → midpoint of the range** is the standard
  interpretation/practice (GAAP Dynamics IAS 37 overview; RSM and Deloitte IFRS↔GAAP comparisons).
- **Large population of similar obligations → expected value** (probability-weighted average;
  EFRAG IAS 37 research summary). Single obligations: often most-likely outcome.

### (2) US GAAP ASC 450

- Accrue when **probable** (~75% practice threshold, higher than IFRS >50%) and reasonably
  estimable.
- Range with a most-likely amount → that amount. Range with **no better estimate → accrue the
  MINIMUM of the range** (RSM, Deloitte comparisons) — explicitly more conservative than the
  IFRS midpoint.

### (3) Prudence / conservatism

- IFRS Conceptual Framework (2018): "cautious prudence" — caution under uncertainty, but
  information must remain **neutral**; prudence does **not** permit systematic bias. Deliberate
  low-end picks violate neutrality unless the standard explicitly mandates them (as ASC 450 does).
- Conservatism is implemented through **asymmetric recognition thresholds**, not biased
  measurement.

### (4) Football-club practice

- No football-specific IFRS: IAS 38 for registrations (capitalise + amortise), IAS 37 for
  uncertain outflows, UEFA licensing requires IFRS/national GAAP + disclosure, does not rewrite
  measurement.
- Conditional add-ons (buying club): recognize as liability + cost of registration when the
  trigger is probable/met; otherwise **contingent liability — disclosed, not recognized**.
- Sell-on/add-on income (selling club): **contingent asset — recognized only when virtually
  certain**, disclosed when probable.
- Portfolio of add-ons across a squad → expected-value provisioning is realistic practice.
